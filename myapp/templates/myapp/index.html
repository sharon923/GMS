WHAT DO WE DO WITH THE WASTE?

Waste disposal refers to the systematic process of managing and disposing of waste materials in a safe, efficient, and environmentally responsible manner. As human activities continue to generate a significant amount of waste, proper waste disposal is crucial to prevent adverse effects on human health, ecosystems, and the overall environment

One of the key challenges in waste disposal is the variety and complexity of waste materials. Waste can be classified into different categories such as solid waste, liquid waste, hazardous waste, biomedical waste, electronic waste, and more. Each type of waste requires specific handling and disposal methods to minimize its potential negative impacts.

After waste segregation, the next step is treatment or processing. This step aims to reduce the volume and hazardous nature of waste. Treatment methods include composting, anaerobic digestion, incineration, and various chemical and physical processes. These methods help to convert waste into less harmful forms or recover valuable resources.
